About Me

When I hear "Accessibility"

Accessibility is something that I find very important in today's society. When it comes down to it, a lack of accessibility is rooted almost entirely in laziness and corporate greed. Businesses don't want to have to spend more money than they thing they "have" to. But in today's world, that is just not acceptable. We need to do better for our fellow humans to make the world better and more accessible.

Automated Tools are NOT enough

Automated tools can be useful. It is a great starting off point to find very obvious flaws. But they should not be seen as a be all end all. We can use these tools in conjunction with our knowledge of accessibility to create more accessible websites.

Acessibilty Standards I Use

WCAG Guidelines are one thing that is cruicial in understanding accessible web content. WCAG 2.1 is the current standard, and WCAG 2.2 is currently being finalized. In order to help achieve these standards, W3C has guides which give helpful pointers and instructions.

Why Inclusion During Design Matters

I have seen first hand what it's like to find work arounds and solve problems that arise from designing for the averages. Being 6'9", while I acknowledge that I do not have to deal with the problems that many disabled people do, I can sympathize as someone that lives in a world that was not built for their personal challenges. It is because of this that I would love to live in a world that embraces people for their differences and designs inclusively so that everyone can have the same amount of joy and excitement over their every day life.

My Accessibilty Statement

This is an accessibility statement from Travis Howey

Measures to support accessibilty

We take the following measures to ensure accessibility:

  • Include accessibility as part of our mission statement.
  • Include accessibility throughout our internal policies.
  • Provide continual accessibility training for our staff.
  • Assign clear accessibility goals and responsibilities.

Conformance status

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) defines requirements for designers and developers to improve accessibility for people with disabilities. It defines three levels of conformance: Level A, Level AA, and Level AAA. WEBSITE_NAME is partially conformant with WCAG 2.1 level AA. Partially conformant means that some parts of the content do not fully conform to the accessibility standard.
